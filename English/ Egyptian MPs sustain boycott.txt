<p>Egyptians did not see the walkout from parliament because television cameras had been banned.</p></div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">The government argues that the amendments will enable the state of emergency, in place since the 1981 assassination of Anwar Sadat, the previous president, to be lifted.</div>
</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">But Amnesty International has said the amendments &#x201C;write into the permanent law emergency-style powers that have been used to violate human rights over more than two decades&#x201D;.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"><strong>Rubber stamp</strong></div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">On Tuesday, the Egyptian parliament is expected to rubber stamp Mubarak&#x2019;s proposed amendments before they are put to a popular referendum on April 4.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">The ruling National Democratic party (NDP) has a majority of seats in the Egyptian parliament large enough to pass the 34 amendments.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">But without the participation of most opposition parties, the NDP has been accused of lacking the moral authority to implement such changes.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">Egypt&#x2019;s opposition has already denounced the changes as &#x201C;opening the way to a police state&#x201D;.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">The Muslim Brotherhood, candidates of which constitute Egypt&#x2019;s main opposition, has said it will boycott the vote.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">Other opposition parties have said they will vote against the proposed amendments.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">Describing the reaction to the proposals, Amr El-Khaky, Al Jazeera&#x2019;s Cairo correspondent, said this was &#x201C;the first time in modern history that all opposition groups have come to an understanding&#x201D;.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"><strong>Amendments criticised</strong></div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">Critics of the government say the amendments are designed to pre-empt any further gains by the opposition and will pave the way for Gamal, Mubarak&#x2019;s son, to inherit power from his father.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">The proposed amendment to Article 179 has come in for particular criticism since it would allow the authorities to arrest suspects, search their homes, read their mail and tap their phones without a warrant.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">It would also mean civilians accused of &#x201C;terrorism&#x201D; could be tried in military courts.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">The change to Article 88, which governs elections, is also controversial because it repeals the judicial supervision of the ballot boxes.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">Many believe the judiciary&#x2019;s supervision over the election process allowed for greater representation of opposition MPs in recent elections.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">But, in an interview published on Saturday with <em>Akhbar Al-Youm</em>, a government newspaper, Mubarak defended his amendments.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">He said the reforms were &#x201C;meant to deal with terrorism-related crimes only, through judiciary channels, and the judicial authority&#x201D;.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">Mustafa al-Feki, a member of Egyptian parliament and chairman of the Egyptian People&#x2019;s Assembly foreign affairs committee, said: &#x201C;Not all the opposition has left, but I&#x2019;m not saying everything is rosy.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#x201C;I wish the Muslim Brotherhood had insisted to stay and expressed their ideas and views.</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</div>
<div style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#x201C;Those who are already members of parliament have the right to say what they want, have it published and reject what they want to reject. Democracy is a kind of balance between different kinds of groups.&#x201D;</div>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">Iranian Vice President Muhammad Ali Abtahi told Aljazeera on Tuesday that Iran and Egypt had decided to restore full diplomatic relations, though a minister in Cairo struck a more cautious note.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"></span>&#xA0;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#x201C;The decision to restore relations has been taken,&#x201D; Abtahi said. &#x201C;And in the coming days, insha Allah (God willing), we will see the resumption of our relations.&#x201D; </span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"></span>&#xA0;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"></span><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">An Iranian official earlier told Aljazeera correspondent in Tehran b</span><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">oth countries would make an official announcement within the next day or two once certain administrative procedures had been finalised.&#xA0;</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"></span>&#xA0;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">The move comes after Tehran&#x2019;s city council passed a resolution, asking for the name of the central Khalid al-Islambuli Avenue to be changed. </span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt">The&#xA0;street honoured&#xA0;the man who shot Sadat dead in 1981. <span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">A large mural of al-Islambuli, recognised as a &#x201C;martyr&#x201D; for his opposition to the Egypt-Israeli peace deal, looks down from a towering building on the busy street.</span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"></span>&#xA0;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#x201C;Relations between the two countries are on the right track,&#x201D; </span>said Foreign Minister Ahmid Mahir&#xA0;on Tuesday. The minister said the&#xA0;change of the street name was &#x201C;a positive step but it is not only a matter of renaming a street&#x201D;. </span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"></span><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"><br>Ties between Cairo and Tehran were severed about 25 years ago after Egypt signed a peace deal with Israel and gave asylum to Iran&#x2019;s deposed shah.&#xA0;</span></p>

<p></p><p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#x201C;Relations with Egypt must now be restored, because this will help the Palestinian people, and this is the wish of all Palestinian groups, notably the jihadi groups,&#x201D; said spokesman Hamid Riza Asifi.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"></span>&#xA0;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">The city council noted a decision had already been taken two years ago to rename the street Intifada Avenue, in honour of the Palestinian uprisings. </span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">However,&#xA0;some media have reported the name may honour Muhammad al-Dura, the young Palestinian boy who was filmed being shot dead by Israeli occupation troops in Gaza&#xA0;as he and his father sought cover.</span></p>
<p></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"></span>&#xA0;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"><strong>Policy shift</strong></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"></span>&#xA0;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">Asifi&#xA0;earlier told the Tehran council of a need for a &#x201C;positive initiative on the part of the Islamic republic&#x201D; towards Egypt in light of the &#x201C;new regional situation, positive signals on the part of the Egyptian leadership and circumstances favourable to the development of bilateral relations&#x201D;.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#xA0;</span></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">&#x201C;We now consider that the conditions have been met&#x201D; for restoring full diplomatic relations, Asifi said on Tuesday.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"></span>&#xA0;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">Asifi also praised comments by Egypt&#x2019;s foreign minister in which he said the 1978 Camp David peace deal with Israel was &#x201C;merely a thing of the past&#x201D;.</span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana"></span>&#xA0;</p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN:0cm 0cm 0pt"><span lang="EN-GB" style="FONT-SIZE:10pt;FONT-FAMILY:Verdana">Relations between the two countries were particularly bad when Egypt supported Iraq during its 1980-1988 war against Iran, but trade and other ties have been improving since the 1990s.</span></p>
